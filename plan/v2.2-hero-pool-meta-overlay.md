# PRD — Hero-pool vs OverFast meta overlay

**Status:** Proposed (V2.2)
**Owner:** unassigned
**Related:** [PR #20](https://github.com/jhiggins-tech/OW-Live-Report/pull/20) (OverFast hero meta on player leaderboard); [V2 master plan](./v2.md).

## Context

PR #20 introduced `useHeroMeta()` — a TanStack-cached fetch of OverFast's
`/heroes` and `/heroes/stats` endpoints. The data lives in the client cache but
the Overview's **Team hero pool** bar chart (`HeroPoolBar`) ignores it. Today
that chart shows team playtime by hero; without context it's hard to tell
whether the team is gravitating toward meta-popular picks or running niche
compositions.

## Goals

- On the Overview's Team hero pool bar chart, show each hero's **OverFast
  community pickrate** alongside the team's **playtime share**, so a viewer
  can read off three regimes at a glance:
  - High team time **and** high meta pickrate → meta cookie-cutter.
  - High team time, low meta pickrate → team-specific niche pick.
  - Low team time, high meta pickrate → "the rest of competitive plays
    this, this team doesn't".
- No additional InfluxDB or OverFast network calls — reuse the existing
  `useHeroMeta()` cache.

## Non-goals

- Changes to per-player charts (PR #20 already covers the leaderboard).
- Filtering or normalisation by role / rank / region. The OverFast stats query
  is fixed to `platform=pc, gamemode=competitive, region=americas` in PR #20;
  follow that until someone asks for parameterisation.
- Time-series of meta pickrate. OverFast returns a single current value;
  trending meta evolution is out of scope.

## Proposed approach

1. `HeroPoolBar.tsx` calls `useHeroMeta()` in addition to the existing
   `fetchTeamHeroPool` query. Both are independently cached, both can render
   with skeletons while pending.
2. The bar chart gets a second numeric series: `metaPickrate` per hero.
   - Option A (preferred): a second horizontal bar in a different colour, on
     the same row as the team's bar. Two bars per hero, both on a 0–100%
     normalised scale (team-share-of-team-playtime vs community-pickrate).
   - Option B: scatter overlay — team bar as today, plus a dot per hero
     indicating community pickrate. Less visually clear, skip.
3. Team's playtime is currently shown as raw hours. Re-normalise to
   "team's share of pool" so the two series share an axis (both 0–100%).
   Keep raw hours in the tooltip.
4. Tooltip lines: team hours · team share % · community pickrate % · delta
   (`team − community` percentage points, colour-coded the same way the
   leaderboard delta is in PR #20).

## Open questions

- **Sort key.** Today the bar is sorted by team playtime descending. Should
  we keep that, or switch to "biggest delta vs meta" to highlight the
  most-team-specific picks? Default: keep team playtime descending so the
  chart's primary axis stays meaningful.
- **OverFast outage handling.** If `useHeroMeta()` errors, fall back to the
  current single-bar chart — degrade gracefully rather than failing.

## Risks

- Visual clutter: two bars per row × 15 heroes makes the chart noisier. Cap
  to top-N heroes by team playtime (already capped at `TOP_HERO_COUNT × 3`).
- OverFast pickrate is global, team data is region-specific. A bar's apparent
  delta partly reflects regional meta differences. Note this in the tooltip
  copy ("meta = OverFast americas/pc/competitive").

## Acceptance criteria

- Overview Team hero pool chart shows both team-playtime bars and community
  pickrate, on the same percent axis.
- Tooltip exposes team hours, team share, community pickrate, delta.
- Cold load doesn't regress: no new HTTP requests beyond what PR #20 already
  fires; if `useHeroMeta` is already cached, no requests at all.
- OverFast outage: chart still renders with the team series.

## Estimated effort

~1 PR, ~50–80 LOC mostly in `HeroPoolBar.tsx`. No build / CI changes.
